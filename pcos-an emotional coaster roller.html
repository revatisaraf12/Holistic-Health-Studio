"Pcos - an Emotional Roller Coaster" by Dr.Nikita Kulkarni

The reason to take up this topic is to help Women throughout the globe to help them cope up with Mental stress related to PCOS which will help them to create a sustainable path to freedom. 20 - 25 % of Indian women of childbearing age are suffering from PCOS. Out of which 60% of women are obese, 35 - 40% have fatty liver. 70% have insulin resistance. 60 - 70% have high level of androgen and 40 - 60% have glucose tolerance. Any endocrine ( hormonal) disorder or imbalance affects the entire body, especially the brain where mood glitches can occur. There is also a strong connection between PCOS and insulin resistance which adversely affects other body organs and system. Slightest disturbance of testosterone which women need in small amounts can cause aggressiveness, irritability and Depression. This testosterone imbalance is a key factor in PCOS symptoms. Since endocrine system manages all hormonal functions PCOS sufferers face host of disorders. Most recently the connection between gut health & brain health has come to forefront of PCOS research. How is PCOS diagnosed - Rotterdam criteria for PCOS - If a woman 1) Irregular or absent periods 2) Signs of excessive androgens ( Acne or unusual hairgrowth) or loss, 3) Polycystic ovaries on Sonography PCOS is considered as an emotional roller coaster because of following - 1) The loss of feminity - With PCOS, you have hormonal imbalance with too many male hormones, which gives excess facial and body hair. Excess fat settles in abdomen giving the middle aged male body. When you add in Acne and hair loss on head, this brings shame and loss of feminity 2) Impact of PCOS on relationship - Everyone around you is affected by PCOS. They may see you as difficult, moody, non - participatory and unreasonable. Extra testosterone messes up Sex drive either to make it slump or be non - existent or to kick into over drive. Weight gain and unwanted hair affects intimacy because of shame and self consciousness. Treatment approach - Consult Gynaecologist/ Endocrinologist for expert opinion but conventional medicine does'nt have much to offer. PCOS is a lifestyle disorder which can be well managed with simple changes in your diet, exercise.
